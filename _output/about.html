About This Program

Program Overview

The Somali Fish Stock Training Program is a comprehensive capacity-building initiative to establish evidence-based fisheries management across Somalia’s 6 coastal regions.

Why This Program?

Somalia faces critical challenges in fisheries:

  • Limited scientific capacity for stock assessment
  • Inconsistent data collection across regions
  • Weak policy-science linkages
  • Unsustainable fishing practices
  • Food security concerns

This program directly addresses these gaps through practical, science-based training.

Partnership with TAFIRI

Tanzania Fisheries Research Institute (TAFIRI) brings:

  • 10+ years of Indian Ocean fisheries expertise
  • Proven training and mentoring approaches
  • Regional experience with similar programs
  • Long-term commitment to sustainability

Learning Approach

Theory + Practice

  • Classroom modules establish scientific foundations
  • Field exercises connect theory to real-world monitoring
  • Research vessel training demonstrates advanced techniques
  • Strategy development ensures practical implementation

Participatory Learning

  • Participants from all 6 regions
  • Exchange of regional experiences
  • Peer learning and networking
  • Local solutions to local challenges

Evidence-Based

  • Training grounded in best international practices
  • Use of real Somali fisheries data
  • Attention to local context and constraints
  • Realistic, implementable approaches

3-Year Implementation Plan

2024-2025: Training, capacity building, system design
2025-2026: Deploy monitoring, begin data collection
2026-2027: Scale up, integrate data, conduct assessments
2027+: Sustain, expand, advance to research

Vision 2027

By 2027, Somalia will have coordinated monitoring at all 6 regions, standardized protocols, real-time data sharing, monthly assessments, and demonstrated policy integration - supporting sustainable fisheries and food security for the Somali people.
