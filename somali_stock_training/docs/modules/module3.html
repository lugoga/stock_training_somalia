Overview

Standardized, high-quality data collection is essential for effective stock assessment. This module covers practical protocols for monitoring fisheries.

Learning Objectives

By the end of this module, participants will:

Sampling Design Principles

Key Concepts

  • Population: Target of inference (all landings in a region)
  • Sampling: Efficient way to estimate population characteristics
  • Stratification: Dividing population into meaningful groups
  • Random selection: Reducing bias in sampling

Sampling Types

  • Systematic sampling (every nth landing)
  • Stratified sampling (by vessel type, season, species)
  • Purposive sampling (typical vessels)
  • Adaptive sampling (responsive to conditions)

Landing Site Monitoring

Standard Protocol

  1. Pre-landing observation - vessel and crew info
  2. Catch landing - species and weight recording
  3. Length sampling - take random sample for measurement
  4. Biological sampling - collect specimens for analysis
  5. Data recording - digital or paper forms

Quality Assurance Checks

  • Duplicate measurements
  • Data validation rules
  • Supervisor verification
  • Regular audits

Observer Programs

Onboard Observation

  • Vessel selection
  • Observer training
  • Data collection at sea
  • Safety protocols
